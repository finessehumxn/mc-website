Game, Set, Cap Table: Serena Williams' Second Act as a Mogul

She's the greatest of all time on the court. But her boldest power move might be the one she's making in venture capital and fashion. Here's Serena 2.0.

Serena WilliamsSerena Ventures & S by Serena
Photo: SWinxy · CC BY 4.0 · Wikimedia

When Serena Williams stepped back from professional tennis, the obvious question was: what could possibly top 23 Grand Slam singles titles? The answer, it turns out, was never going to be another trophy. It was a cap table. Because while everyone was watching her serve, Serena was quietly building a business empire designed to last way longer than any match.

The Come-Up: Greatness Was Always the Warm-Up

Serena's origin story is the stuff of legend, two sisters from Compton, hand-coached by their father on public courts, who went on to redefine an entire sport. That relentlessness, the same fire that powered her through decades of dominance, never needed tennis to exist. It just needed a new arena. And Serena found two: venture capital and fashion.

She'd been investing for years before most people noticed, quietly backing startups long before "athlete investor" became a buzzword. By the time she formalized Serena Ventures, she wasn't a celebrity dabbling. She was a serious player with a thesis.

The Creativity: Funding the Founders Everyone Else Ignored

Here's the move that separates Serena from the pack of famous people slapping their names on funds. Serena Ventures built its strategy around backing overlooked founders, women, people of color, and entrepreneurs that traditional venture capital has historically passed over. She reportedly raised a substantial fund and has invested in a wide and growing portfolio of companies, putting capital where her convictions are.

She didn't just want a seat at the table. She built a new table, and started handing out chairs to people the industry kept ignoring.

Then there's S by Serena, her fashion line, where the creativity gets personal. As an athlete who never quite fit the mold the industry expected, Serena designed clothing meant to celebrate strong, confident bodies of all shapes. The brand world is unapologetically bold, inclusive, and built around the idea that style and power aren't opposites. It's fashion with a thesis statement, just like her fund.

The Growth: Building Generational Wealth, On Purpose

Serena has been open about her goal of building generational wealth and influence, not just a paycheck. Her business interests span venture capital, fashion, media, and a portfolio of ventures and endorsements that have reportedly pushed her net worth into the territory that makes her one of the wealthiest athletes-turned-moguls of her era.

What's striking is how intentional it all is. She's spoken about the staggering statistic that only a tiny fraction of venture funding goes to women-led companies, and she's treating that gap not as a tragedy to lament but as a market inefficiency to exploit. That's a founder's brain. That's someone who sees opportunity where others see a closed door.

The court made her famous. The boardroom is making her something arguably more durable: a builder, a backer, and a blueprint for what an athlete's second act can look like when it's driven by vision instead of vanity.

The kicker: She spent a career winning trophies. Now she's spending her capital making sure other people get to win too.
